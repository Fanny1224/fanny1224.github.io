My research at Bambi Group at Fudan university is mainly about utilizing the X-ray data to study the evolution of the system containing a black hole, an accretion disk and a corona (see the figure below). In the case of stellar mass black holes, the thermal radiation from the disk produces a spectrum peaking in the soft X-ray band. The electrons of the corona, which refers to some hot plasma distributed near the black hole, are inversed Compton scattered off by the photons from the disk. Some of these Comptonized photons reaches the infinity, while some can illuminate the accretion disk, and thus produce the reflection spectrum. There are many different models describing the spectral components of this system. We try to use these models to fit the X-ray data from observations, and analyze the evolution of these fitting parameters to see if we can have a better understanding of the properties of the system and how it is changing during and outburst.

An illustration of the black hole - disk - corona model

In my first research, we focus on the HXMT observations of the source MAXI J1820, analyzing the temporal evolution of the spectral parameters in the whole outburst with more than 150 observations. We employ two different models to fit the thermal spectrum of the disk: the Newtonian model diskbb and the relativistic model nkbb. These two models provide different pictures of the source in the soft state, including the change of the corona and the inner disk radius of the source. However, if we change some of the fixed parameters, it is possible to find a consistency between two different models, including the measurement of the black hole spin.
