Hi, My name is Ningyue Fan 范宁玥 (Fanny). I am currently an undergraduate student of the Department of Physics, Fudan University, Shanghai, China. My supervisor is Professor Cosimo Bambi at Fudan University and Professor Jiajun Zhang at Shanghai Astronomical Observatory (SHAO). I am interested in utilizing X-ray spectrum to study the accretion process of the black hole, as well as using machine learning as a tool to perform some tasks like foreground removal in Astrophysics and Cosmology.


I grew up in Shanghai, a bustling city with a fast-paced lifestyle. However, I actually prefer the leisurely countryside life. Therefore, during holidays, I often visit my grandparents' homes in the countryside of Fujian and Jiangsu Province with my parents. Both of them are university teachers, and it is thanks to their guidance and inspiration that I developed a strong curiosity for the natural sciences since childhood, which eventually led me to choose physics as my major in university. I also have a little brother who is 15 years younger than me.


Besides the academic atmosphere in my family, the beauty of clear and precise formalism in physics, and the pleasure I find in exploring new things make me devoted to doing scientific research as my career. Initially, I was uncertain about which field to choose. However, after trying astrophysics, I found it really cool. Analyzing data from black holes or studying reionization in the far reaches of the universe feels like bringing to life the fantastic dreams I had while reading science-fiction and popular science books during my childhood. Although our research involves some kind of dirty or repeated work, it is exciting to gain a deeper understanding of the underlying physical processes step by step.
